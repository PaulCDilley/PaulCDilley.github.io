Paul Dilley is a scholar of the Religions of Late Antiquity, in particular ancient Christianity and Manichaeism. He also has expertise in Digital Humanities and Humanistic AI.

He is Associate Professor in the Department of Classics and Religious Studies, and a member of the Center for the Book, at the University of Iowa.

Research and Teaching
======
Dilley's research focuses on the literary, social, and material history of religious communities in Late Antiquity, especially early Christian and Manichaean monasticism and book culture. His Digital Humanities work brings computational and AI-driven methods to the study of ancient texts, including the development of digital tools and resources for working with under-resourced ancient languages and corpora.

Use the tabs above to find resources related to his current projects, including Lexical Topography, Experimental Metrics for Textual Diffusion, the Gospel of Thomas, and resources for Coptic Dialects.
